CV – Adriana Calahorra-Oliart

Adriana Calahorra-Oliart

Education

PhD Biological Sciences. 2022. Mastozoology Museum, Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM).

Candidature essay: Use of morphological, molecular and niche characters in evolutionary studies and their integration for phylogenetic inferences and species delimitation in bats.

Thesis: Diversification of Glossophaga soricina in Mesoamerica.

MSc Evolutionary Biology. 2016. Institute of Ecology, UNAM.

BSc Biology. 2013. UNAM.

Certifications

Data Science and Machine Learning, 2022. Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

Research Experience

Postdoctoral Fellowship (2024–2025)

  • Demographic history of rhesus macaque.
  • Patterns of X chromosomal vs autosomal diversity in aye-ayes.
  • Recombination rate estimation along rhesus macaque autosomes.

Postdoctoral Fellowship (2023–2024)

  • Morphological convergence between domestic dogs and phyllostomid bats.
  • Building a bioreactor for microbiome studies.

Doctoral Researcher (2016–2022)

  • Taxonomic resolution of a cryptic bat species complex using geometric morphometrics, ecological niche modeling and phylogenies.
  • Elevated four subspecies to species level based on findings.

Master Researcher (2013–2015)

  • Evolution of plant defense traits in Datura stramonium.
  • Showed introduced populations invest up to ten times less in defense traits.

Undergraduate Researcher (2011–2012)

  • Designed a program to teach natural selection in high school.
  • Developed online content for teaching evolutionary biology.
